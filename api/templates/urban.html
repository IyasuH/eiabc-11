Urban

The undergraduate program of Urban & Regional Planning is designed to prepare students for careers in the field of urban and regional planning. The program typically lasts for four years and leads to a Bachelor of Science (BS) degree. During the program, students learn about the history, theory, and practice of urban and regional planning, as well as the social, economic, and environmental factors that shape the built environment. They also develop skills in spatial analysis, mapping, and community engagement. Students learn how to create plans and policies that promote sustainable and equitable development, and they have the opportunity to work on real-world planning projects. Graduates of the program are well-equipped to pursue careers in urban planning, community development, environmental planning, transportation planning, and related fields, or to continue their education in graduate programs in planning or related fields.
