Arch

The undergraduate program of Architecture is a five-year program that prepares students for careers in the field of architecture. The program combines coursework in design, technology, history, and theory to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the field. Students learn how to design buildings, landscapes, and urban spaces that are functional, aesthetically pleasing, and sustainable. They also develop skills in computer-aided design (CAD), model-making, and construction documentation. In addition, students study the history and theory of architecture, which provides them with a context for understanding the social, cultural, and political forces that shape the built environment. Graduates of the program are well-prepared to pursue careers in architecture, landscape architecture, urban design, and related fields, or to continue their education in graduate programs in architecture or related fields.
